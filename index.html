Why LuaForge exists

Everyone has a game in them. Almost no one ships it.

Roblox Studio proved that millions of people will build games if the barrier to entry is a drag-and-drop editor instead of a compiler. But Roblox Studio ends on Roblox's platform, under Roblox's economy.

LuaForge takes the same no-code philosophy — node-graph logic, a familiar docked editor, instant play-testing — and points it at the open PC market. Build a game with zero lines of code, then ship it straight to Steam, keep the lion's share of the revenue, and own the audience you built.

"You built a game. Now ship it to Steam." — that's the whole pitch.
